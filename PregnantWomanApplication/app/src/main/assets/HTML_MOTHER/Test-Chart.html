Test Chart

Below is a recommended schedule for tests performed during pregnancy. The tests aim to provide basic information while tracking a routine pregnancy. Consult your nurse or physician if you have any questions.

1. Urine or Blood Test :

Around the time of the delayed menstruation, a urine or blood test can be performed to determine pregnancy.

2. Ultra Sound Test :

At 7-8 weeks a woman should have an ultrasound to determine the location of the pregnancy sac, size and vitality (heartbeat) of the foetus.

3. Routine Tests :

At 10 weeks your doctor will give you a follow-up card to track your pregnancy.
Routine Tests: Blood count, fasting glucose, urine culture, blood type rubella antibodies, VDRL, CMV, antibodies, toxoplasma and HBsAG and if necessary an HIV test.

4. Early Ultrasound Screen Test :

Week 15-16: early ultrasound screening test, An ultrasound scan sends sound waves through your womb (uterus). These waves bounce off your baby as echoes.

5. Triple Screen Test :

Week 17: Triple screen test, fetoprotein or amniocentesis and blood count.The results of the blood test can help your doctor see if your baby may be at higher risk for certain birth defects such as Down syndrome and neural tube defects.

6. Late Ultrasound Anomaly Scan :

Week 22-25: late ultrasound anomaly , An anomaly scan takes a close look at your baby and your uterus (womb). The person carrying out the scan (sonographer) will check that your baby is developing normally.

7. Glucose Tolerance Test :

Week 26: A glucose tolerance test is done if a glucose screening test result is higher than normal. It is used to diagnose gestational diabetes. Gestational diabetes is high blood sugar (diabetes) that starts or is found during pregnancy.

Following Point discussion or review with Maternity Doctor, Except Last Point

8. Discussion about Last Test(Glucose Tolerance Test) :

Week 28:A visit to the physician to discuss glucose test results and talk about receiving RH immune globulin if RH negative.

9. Review for Fatal Growth :

Week 28-30:review of fatal growth of stages during pregnancy.

10. Consult With Maternity Doctor :

Week 32: for regular follow-up visit.

11. Consult With Maternity Doctor for Weight Growth :

Week 34-39:weekly tracking of fatal growth weight.

12. Consult about Ultrasound Test :

Week 40:follow-up with doctor, ultrasound, monitoring biophysical profile every 2-4 days.

13. Stress Test :

Week 41-42:referral to the delivery room to perform a stress test or induce labor.The contraction stress test — also called a stress test or an oxytocin challenge test — may be done during pregnancy to measure the baby's heart rate during uterine contractions. Its purpose is to make sure the baby can get the oxygen he needs from the placenta during labor.
